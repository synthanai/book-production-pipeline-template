# blueprints/

**Tier:** 2 (Structural Specifications)
**Purpose:** Detailed plans for specific sections (acts, parts, volumes)

---

## üìã Overview

Blueprints are detailed structural plans for major sections of your book (typically acts, parts, or volumes). They apply architectures and master concepts to specific portions of your narrative.

**Authority:** Tier 2 - Section-specific structure

---

## üìÇ Typical Contents

### Act Blueprints (for multi-act structure)
- Act I Blueprint
- Act II Blueprint
- Act III Blueprint
- Act IV Blueprint (if applicable)

### Part Blueprints (for non-fiction)
- Part I Blueprint (Foundations)
- Part II Blueprint (Application)
- Part III Blueprint (Advanced)

### Volume Blueprints (for series)
- Book 1 Blueprint
- Book 2 Blueprint
- Book 3 Blueprint

---

## üìä What Goes in a Blueprint

### Structural Overview
- Section purpose and function
- Key themes and focus
- Character arcs in this section
- Plot progression
- Pacing specifications

### Chapter Organization
- Chapter-by-chapter breakdown
- Chapter purposes and goals
- Scene requirements per chapter
- Word count targets
- Transition specifications

### Story Progression
- Plot points in this section
- Character development milestones
- Thematic evolution
- Conflict escalation/resolution
- Framework integration points

### Quality Criteria
- Section-specific standards
- Validation requirements
- Success metrics
- Cohesion requirements

## üéØ Best Practices

1. **Align with architecture** - Follow narrative architecture specs
2. **Detail chapters** - Each chapter purpose clear
3. **Track arcs** - Character and plot progression specified
4. **Set targets** - Word counts and pacing guidelines
5. **Plan transitions** - How sections connect
6. **Define validation** - Section-specific quality criteria

---

## üí° When to Create Blueprints

### Create blueprints if:
- Multi-act or multi-part structure
- Complex plot with many threads
- Multiple character arcs to track
- Series or trilogy planning
- Want detailed chapter roadmap

### Skip blueprints if:
- Simple linear narrative
- Short form (articles, essays)
- Exploratory writing style
- Minimal planning preferred

---

## üìä Typical Size

- 30,000 - 50,000 words per blueprint
- 3-4 blueprints for typical novel structure
- More detailed than architectures for specific section
- Less comprehensive than master story blueprint
